A group of enthusiastic photographers founded AIUB Photography Club (AIUBPC) 17 years ago. AIUBPC has gone a long way from its early days in the hallways of AIUB's Banani Campus to being one of the most popular student-run photography organizations throughout Bangladesh. In order to assist one another in becoming better photographers, we at AIUBPC believe in stimulating enthusiasm, sharing knowledge, and sharpening the photographic eye. Our photographers have made a lasting impression well beyond the borders of our nation throughout the years, demonstrating their intense passion for the form and the AIUBPC ideology. By setting up Tale of The Darkroom III, an international photography exhibition, AIUBPC has decided to take the voyage to a more promising level in order to add greater significance to photographic history.

AIUBPC started organizing exhibitions from 2011. The first time it was Intra AIUB Photography Exhibition (IAPE), during the Fall 2010-11 semester. Second time was in the year 2012 holding the same name, Intra AIUB Photography Exhibition (IAPE). It was a fabulous and spectacular event and by all means surpassing its predecessor. Third time was a national level exhibition named Bangladesh Inter University Photography Exhibition – BIUPE 2013 at Dhaka Art Center. Fourth time was the season 2 of BIUPE, Bangladesh Inter University Photography Exhibition – Season 2 at Drik Gallery.

In 2017, AIUB Photography Club organized “Tale of the Darkroom” which was the first photography exhibition where all the colleges and university students shared a common platform to showcase their best photographs. “Tale of the Darkroom II” was the second season of the previous exhibition where the participation was even higher. It was organized in 2018.

After the pandemic and the switch from online to campus classes in 2022, AIUBPC announced its 10th Executive Committee. The Executive panel kicked off the semester by planning the first Intra event for all AIUB students, alumni, and staffs, which was the "Intra AIUB Photography Exhibition (IAPE) - Season III".

Tale of The Darkroom III

Traditional film photography uses a specialized area called a "darkroom" to develop and print photos. The reason it is referred to as a "darkroom" is that it must be absolutely dark to prevent any light from damaging the light-sensitive film or paper used to record the image. To bring out the picture on the film, photographers utilize a process called "developing" in the darkroom. The film must be "fixed" after development in order to preserve the image's quality. Although using a darkroom involves a lot of time and skill, many photographers appreciate the hands-on aspect of the process and the opportunity to fine-tune their pictures. Despite the widespread use of digital photography, a sizable number of photographers still like the distinct look of classic film photography and keep their darkrooms in operation. We're going to have a particular darkroom to create the mood of vintage photography, which will draw visitors in with something unique compared to other exhibitions.

Our Goal

The exhibition is intended to be the capstone event for the current committee, thus this year we are aiming to have a different, worldwide exhibition with participation encouraged from all photographers in Bangladesh, with a focus on high school and college students. This exhibition offers a clear window through which one can look into the modern world.

Without your participation, we won't be able to host our first international event. As we advance in time, join us in this big quest and be a witness to this amazing photographic event.

    • About AIUBPC

    Slogan: A Window To See The World

    Foundation Day: 24th December 2005

    Management: OSA, American International University-Bangladesh

    Here, students from different departments enter the club to learn, practice and develop their photography skills. Besides photography, they learn about leadership, communication skills, and how to organize an event. The club arranges workshops and seminars to build the photography skills of its members. With this knowledge, students from the club gain the skills required to cover different events held at the university (both on-campus and off-campus). They participate in national and international photography exhibitions and bring prizes that portray their passion and talent for photography. In addition, the club organizes photo walks, picnics, club tours, and photo discussions so that club members can get some on-field experience, remain active and bond with each other. AIUBPC aims to gather photography lovers & enthusiast photographers in one place, develop photography skills through workshops, photo walks and discussions, photo tours, and many more, cover all events of the University, develop leadership skills, and communication skills, and bring out the creativity of club members.
